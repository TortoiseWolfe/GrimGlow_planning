## 1. What Draws Her

### The User's Three Options

**The botany** — "pollen on the forearms" (Turnarounds). "A small pressed flower visible inside" a sample pouch (Turnarounds). The past is alive in a way the future might not be. Victorian London has biology, ecology, unmanaged nature pushing through cobblestones.

**The slower pace** — a world without holographic data feeds, without temporal corridor schedules, without the velocity of the future. 1885 moves at the speed of horses and handwriting. For a scientist, slower means observable.

**Studying history firsthand** — "once-in-a-lifetime research opportunity" (Story Bible). Every scientist's impossible wish: to be present at the moment you study. Luma doesn't just read about the industrial revolution — she's standing in it.
