# GRIMGLOW — Character Visual Prompts

Mid-mission stage. Painterly / illustrated style. Action poses in environment.

---

## Sable — The Captain

```
Painterly illustrated style, rich detail. A tiny humanoid figure, approximately four inches tall, standing on the brass rim of a Victorian gas lamp high above a fog-filled street. Military bearing, weight on her back foot, scanning the city below. She is a woman in her late thirties with sharp features, dark brown skin, and close-cropped silver-white hair — a deliberate choice, not age. Her suit is form-fitting metallic silver, scuffed and streaked with soot along the arms and knees, but still catching light. A faint holographic heads-up display glows in pale blue geometric shapes near her left eye — floating glyphs that read as magical runes. Her wing-pack is stowed as a compact geometric module between her shoulder blades, faintly luminous at the seams. She carries herself with authority that's costing her something. One hand rests on a small sidearm at her hip — a sleek future weapon that would look like a wand to Victorian eyes. The environment dwarfs her: the gas lamp's glass housing is the size of a room behind her, the street below a vast canyon of cobblestone. Warm amber gaslight against cool holographic blue. Oil paint texture, visible brushwork, dramatic chiaroscuro lighting.
```

## Wren — The Engineer

```
Painterly illustrated style, rich detail. A tiny humanoid figure, approximately four inches tall, crouched inside the open back panel of a massive Victorian clockwork mechanism — gears the size of doors surround her. She is a woman in her early thirties with East Asian features, wiry build, dark hair pulled back in a messy functional knot with a few strands fallen loose. Her silver suit is the most worn of the squad — oil-stained at the forearms, copper dust on her knees, a scorch mark along one shoulder — but prismatic light still pulses faintly beneath the grime at her joints and collar. She's working: both hands manipulating a hovering holographic schematic that floats between her and a massive brass gear, the hologram casting structured blue-white light across the tarnished metal. Geometric holographic tools orbit her hands like floating runes. Her expression is focused, lips slightly parted, problem-solving. Her wing-pack is half-deployed — one translucent dragonfly wing extended for balance, its geometric scaffolding visible, the other folded. Steampunk machinery fills the frame around her at enormous scale. Warm brass and copper tones contrasting with cool holographic light. Oil paint texture, visible brushwork, dramatic workshop lighting.
```

## Jink — The Scout

```
Painterly illustrated style, rich detail. A tiny humanoid figure, approximately four inches tall, mid-flight through a Victorian chimney forest — a roofscape of terracotta pots, iron cowls, and billowing smoke. They are the youngest of their squad, late teens or early twenties, with a lean androgynous build, light brown skin, and a shock of dark curly hair kept short on the sides but wild on top. Their expression is a grin — reckless, alive, loving this. Their wing-pack is fully deployed: two pairs of translucent holographic wings shaped like dragonfly wings, rendered in structured light with visible geometric lattice, leaving faint prismatic trails as they bank hard between chimney stacks. Their silver suit is dirty — soot-streaked across the chest, a tear along one forearm patched with what looks like copper wire — but the holographic elements glow bright through the grime, especially along the spine and wing mounts. One hand trails behind them leaving a faint arc of holographic light, the other reaches forward. They're fast and they know it. Smoke and sparks swirl in their wake. The massive Victorian chimneys tower around them like a canyon. Warm firelight from below, cold moonlight from above. Oil paint texture, visible brushwork, dynamic motion blur, sense of speed.
```

## Thresh — The Soldier

```
Painterly illustrated style, rich detail. A tiny humanoid figure, approximately four inches tall, pressed flat against a dark Victorian brick wall in an alley, back to the surface, weapon raised. They are broad-shouldered and muscular for their small stature — the squad's heavy hitter — with pale skin, a square jaw, a military buzz cut of copper-red hair, and eyes that are scanning the shadows with visible fear. This is a soldier who was brave in a different world and is terrified in this one. Their silver suit has armored plating at the shoulders, chest, and forearms — more militarized than the others — scuffed and dented, with a long scratch across the chest plate. Holographic elements glow at the edges of the armor in geometric patterns, pulsing faintly like a heartbeat. Their weapon is a compact rifle-like object that would look like a strange crossbow to Victorian eyes, held in a white-knuckle grip. Their wing-pack is stowed and dark — not flying, not drawing attention. The alley around them is enormous: a drainpipe the width of a tunnel, a discarded bottle the size of a barrel, a rat visible in the middle distance that is functionally the size of a horse. The threat is the scale of everything. Muted dark tones, deep shadow, a single line of gaslight from the street cutting across the frame. Oil paint texture, visible brushwork, tension and claustrophobia.
```

## Luma — The Medic/Scientist

```
Painterly illustrated style, rich detail. A tiny humanoid figure, approximately four inches tall, standing on the open page of an enormous leather-bound book in a Victorian study, examining a botanical illustration that is larger than she is. She is a woman in her late twenties with South Asian features, warm brown skin, large expressive eyes behind delicate holographic lenses that hover just in front of her face — they look like spectral spectacles, glowing faintly with data. Her dark hair is long and braided, threaded with a thin luminous filament that pulses softly. Her silver suit is lightly worn — less damaged than the others because she hangs back from the front lines — but still marked with dust and a few ink stains from the study. She has a holographic medical scanner hovering near her left hand, projecting a small rotating three-dimensional model of a plant cell that casts structured light on the book's page. Her expression is wonder — not fear, not tension, pure fascination. Her wing-pack is stowed but its seams glow warmly. The study around her is vast: inkwell like a cauldron, quill pen like a fallen tree, candlelight casting everything in amber warmth. She is the one who doesn't want to leave. Warm golden tones dominating, holographic cool blue as accent. Oil paint texture, visible brushwork, intimacy and curiosity.
```

## Theodore Edmund Hartley — The Steampunk Boy

```
Painterly illustrated style, rich detail. A Victorian boy of twelve or thirteen, kneeling on the wooden floor of a cramped tinker's workshop, peering into a gap between the floorboards where a faint, impossible light glows up at his face. He is slight, with a narrow face, untidy sandy-brown hair, and wide hazel eyes reflecting the prismatic glow from below. He wears the clothes of a working-class apprentice: a collarless linen shirt with rolled sleeves, a worn leather waistcoat too large for him, patched wool trousers, and scuffed boots. His hands are clever — stained with machine oil, a small burn scar on one knuckle, a brass gear ring on his index finger that he fidgets with. His expression is the exact moment between fear and wonder, mouth slightly open, not yet decided what he's seeing. The workshop surrounds him at human scale: workbenches cluttered with clockwork parts, hanging tools, a half-assembled automaton on a shelf, gaslight warm and smoky. The only cool light in the frame is the holographic glow from between the floorboards — structured, geometric, alien — bleeding up around his fingers and across his face. He is ordinary in an extraordinary moment. Warm workshop tones with that single intrusion of impossible blue-white light. Oil paint texture, visible brushwork, Rembrandt-style lighting, wonder and vulnerability.
```
