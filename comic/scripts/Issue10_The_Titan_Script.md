# GRIMGLOW Issue 10 — "The Titan" — Panel-by-Panel Production Script

---

## Production Notes

- **Degradation state:** Heavy plus battle damage. All suits carry accumulated wear from nine issues — soot, scuffs, dents, improvised repairs, environmental staining — and take additional combat damage during the Titan encounter. Wren's copper suit is the most environmentally worn (oil-stained forearms, copper dust ground into knees, scorch mark on shoulder, new telegraph grease smeared across torso). Jink's emerald suit has the patched forearm tear from earlier issues plus new scoring along the left flank from a glancing Titan strike. Thresh's crimson armor shows pre-existing dents and scratches plus significant new impact damage — chest plate cracked, left pauldron crushed inward, holographic edge-lighting flickering erratically from the Titan's disruption field. Sable's silver suit is soot-darkened and now gains a deep gouge along the right forearm from shrapnel. Luma's sapphire-violet suit remains the least physically damaged but takes telegraph debris hits and shows burn marks from the disruption pulse on her scanner arm. Holographic elements on all suits glow through grime — but during the Titan's disruption field (Pages 8-14), all fairy tech stutters, dims, and dies intermittently. The visual metaphor: the squad is at its lowest point physically, but the light never fully goes out.
- **Lighting baseline:** This issue uses five distinct lighting environments — see section headers for specifics. The dominant progression: warm gaslight for mission setup, warm amber telegraph-office interior for infiltration, sickly amber-black void and disrupted holographics for the Titan encounter, harsh combat lighting for the fight, and grey pre-dawn for aftermath. The Titan ABSORBS light — its presence creates visual voids. Dark iridescence appears only at its edges, where its surface catches ambient light like oil on water.
- **Dialogue style:** Military professionals under extreme stress. Sable commands with authority earned, not assumed — trust is damaged from Issue 9 but crisis restores function. Wren is technical and fast under fire. Jink is sharp and scared. Thresh speaks the least but acts the most — silence becomes courage. Luma reports calmly until the scanner fails. Theodore speaks in period-appropriate Victorian English — the wonder is replaced by real fear for the first time.
- **Key narrative beats:** Mission setup, fractured squad functional (Pages 1-3). Telegraph infiltration and discovery of tampering (Pages 4-7). Titan reveal — full-page splash (Page 7). Disruption field and combat (Pages 8-14). Wren's telegraph solution (Pages 15-17). Aftermath and mythology growth (Pages 18-20). Theodore's fear (Page 21). Dual fairy tale echo — folklorist introduction (Page 22).
- **Panel count target:** ~95 panels across 22 pages.

## SECTION 1: MISSION SETUP (Pages 1–3)

### Lighting Baseline
Warm amber gaslight bleeds through the wall gap into the camp, mixing with depleted holographic perimeter glow. The camp lighting is dimmer than in earlier issues — power cells nearly spent. When the squad moves outside, Victorian gaslit streets dominate in warm amber tones. The cool fairy-tech light is muted, unreliable — a visual reminder that resources are running low. The mood is functional, not comfortable. These people are working together because they must.

### Scene Setup
The wall cavity camp, familiar from Issues 4-9. But the atmosphere is wrong — the spatial fracture from Issue 9 lingers. Characters occupy the same space but at slightly greater distances. Wren has assumed operational leadership for this mission. Sable's authority is present but tentative — trust damaged, not destroyed. The component target is a copper induction coil from a telegraph station, one of the items on Wren's list from Issue 2.
