# GRIMGLOW Issue 5 — "The Clockmaker's Heart" — Panel-by-Panel Production Script

---

## Production Notes

- **Degradation state:** Moderate wear. The squad has been living in the wall cavity for days, running sorties through Victorian London. Wren's copper suit is the dirtiest of the five — oil stains at forearms and elbows, copper dust ground into the knees, a scorch mark on the left shoulder from the crash still visible, new grease smears from camp repair work. Her suit becomes visibly worse during this issue — she picks up fresh oil and copper dust inside the clock mechanism. Sable's silver suit carries a grey soot bloom across the right shoulder and forearms, dust at knees, water marks from Issue 2. Jink's emerald suit has the chest soot streak from Issue 3, dust from scouting, and new chimney soot from the rooftop approach. Thresh's ruby-crimson suit shows the dented left pauldron from Issue 1, leg scuffs, and grime from perching on exposed masonry. Luma's sapphire-violet suit remains the cleanest — dust, ink stains at the hands, minor wear — she stays at camp on comms for this mission. Holographic accent nodes on all suits are slightly dimmer than Issues 1-2 due to power rationing, but still pulse visibly through grime.
- **Lighting baseline:** This issue is dominated by polished brass and warm workshop amber. The clockmaker's shop and its mechanism are the primary visual environment — warm oil lamps, candlelight, polished metal reflecting amber everywhere. Wren's copper-gold holographic tools catching on brass and steel create the dual-light contrast. Exterior scenes use gaslight and overcast grey-gold. The wall-cavity camp retains its cool holographic baseline with warm leaks from the building above.
- **Dialogue style:** Wren leads. She briefs like an engineer — systematic, precise, component-specific. Sable defers to Wren's operational expertise but retains command authority. Jink is restless and ready. Thresh is professional but visibly uneasy with the exposure of an overwatch position. Luma is steady on comms. Theodore speaks period-appropriate Victorian English — earnest, slightly nervous, genuinely enthusiastic about clockwork.
- **Key narrative plants:** Wren's first moment of admiration for "primitive" human engineering. She sees beauty in the clockwork — not condescension, but recognition. This is the emotional turn that shifts her arc. Theodore proves himself operationally useful for the first time — his diversion is clumsy but effective, establishing his role for future missions. The clockmaker is competent and observant — not an antagonist, but a human going about his work who is terrifyingly close to discovering Wren.
- **Scale note:** The interior of the grandfather clock at fairy scale is a cathedral. Polished brass gears the size of doors. Pendulum mechanisms swinging in vast arcs. Mainsprings coiled like industrial cables. Jewel bearings glowing deep red. Everything is precision-engineered, beautiful, and lethal — a character caught between meshing gears would be crushed. This is the KEY VISUAL ENVIRONMENT of the issue.

## SECTION 1: PLANNING (Pages 1-3)

### Lighting Baseline
Wall-cavity camp. Cool holographic glow from perimeter emitters and the power cell on the timber ledge — the established camp lighting from Issues 2-4. Warm filtered gaslight leaks through floorboard gaps above, casting amber bands across the timber beams. Wren's briefing schematic is the dominant light source during the planning sequence — structured copper-gold holographic wireframe filling the center of the camp space, casting geometric shadows across all five faces.

### Environment Setup
The wall-cavity camp between timber beams — the squad's established base. Damaged equipment arranged along ledges, holographic perimeter emitters marking the edges. The space is cramped but organized. For the briefing, Wren has commandeered the central timber beam as a table. Her holographic projector casts a three-dimensional cutaway of the clockmaker's shop in copper-gold light — showing the building layout, the target clock, the gear assembly's location within the mechanism, entry and exit routes, and assigned positions for each squad member. This is Wren's element — she stands at the projection, hands moving through it, building the plan in light.
