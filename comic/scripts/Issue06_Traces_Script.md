# GRIMGLOW Issue 6 — "Traces" — Panel-by-Panel Production Script

---

## Production Notes

- **Degradation state:** Moderate wear, advancing. The squad has been operating in Victorian London for over a week. Luma's sapphire-violet suit has accumulated dust across the shoulders and back, ink stains at the hands and forearms, and a thin film of grime at the knees from crawling through the wall cavity. For this issue she picks up brick dust, basement damp stains, and cobweb residue from the investigation sites. Her holographic lenses remain functional but show a hairline crack in the left lens from Issue 1. Sable's silver suit carries the established grey soot bloom across the right shoulder, forearm dust, water marks, and a new tension-wear crease at the collar from her private file sessions. Wren's copper suit is the squad's dirtiest — oil at forearms and elbows, copper dust at knees, clockwork grease from Issue 5. Jink's emerald suit has the chest soot streak, chimney soot, and new scuffs from scouting runs. Thresh's ruby-crimson suit shows the dented left pauldron, leg scuffs, and accumulated grime. Holographic accent nodes on all suits are noticeably dimmer than earlier issues due to power rationing — they pulse faintly through grime, visible but subdued.
- **Lighting baseline:** Fog dominates this issue. The Victorian city is muted grey-brown, desaturated, oppressive. Warmth comes only from distant gas lamps — small amber punctuation in the grey. Luma's holographic scanner is the primary cool light source: structured blue-violet, precise geometric readouts, casting clean alien light against grimy Victorian surfaces. The Titan shadow, when it appears, is a void — it absorbs light rather than casting or reflecting it, creating a wrongness in the visual field. The basement sequence at the end of this half is lit entirely by Luma's scanner against stone and darkness. The wall-cavity camp retains its established cool holographic baseline with warm leaks from above.
- **Dialogue style:** Luma leads the investigation thread. She briefs like a scientist — methodical, evidence-based, increasingly alarmed as the data accumulates. Theodore speaks period-appropriate Victorian English — earnest, observant, navigating his city with natural confidence. Sable is clipped and controlled in the briefing, giving permission with visible reluctance. Wren asks sharp technical questions. Jink fidgets. Thresh is quiet. When Luma and Theodore are alone in the field, their dialogue is warmer — a partnership developing in real time, the scientist and her giant guide.
- **Key narrative plants:** The Titan evidence escalates from curious to frightening across the investigation sequence. The shadow at the end of the street (Pages 10-11) is the first near-sighting — not a full Titan reveal, but enough wrongness to signal that something is watching. The fairy glyph at human height in the basement (Page 12) connects directly to the glyph Luma found in Issue 4, simplified for human comprehension — someone was teaching. The investigation establishes the evidence base that pays off in Issue 8's cache discovery and Issue 12's synthesis.
- **Scale note:** The Luma-in-Theodore's-pocket sequence is this issue's defining scale image. Luma rides in his waistcoat breast pocket, peeking out over the fabric edge — a four-inch scientist scanning the city from a boy's chest. The tenderness and the absurdity sit together. Theodore navigates at human scale while Luma reads the environment at a level no human eye can reach.

## SECTION 1: LUMA MAPPING ANOMALIES (Pages 1-3)

### Lighting Baseline
Wall-cavity camp. Established cool holographic glow from perimeter emitters and the power cell. Warm amber bands filter through floorboard gaps above. Luma's anomaly map dominates — a structured blue-violet holographic projection of the local city district, pulsing with data nodes that mark each anomaly site. The map casts geometric shadows across the squad's faces and the timber beams. When Luma highlights specific sites, the relevant node brightens, throwing sharper light.

### Environment Setup
The wall-cavity camp between timber beams. The squad is assembled for a briefing, but this time Luma commands the space. Her holographic projector casts a three-dimensional map of the surrounding city district — streets rendered in blue-violet geometric wireframe, with anomaly sites marked as pulsing nodes of brighter light. Connecting lines between the nodes show a convergence pattern — the anomalies are not random. They cluster. The projection floats at the center of the camp, rotating slowly, data annotations scrolling in fairy geometric script at each node. Luma stands beside it, one hand in the projection, the other holding her scanner. The rest of the squad is arranged around the beam — Sable standing, arms crossed; Wren leaning forward, studying the technical data; Jink perched on a higher beam, one leg dangling; Thresh seated, weapon across knees, watchful.
