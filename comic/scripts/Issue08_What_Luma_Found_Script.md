# GRIMGLOW Issue 8 — "What Luma Found" — Panel-by-Panel Production Script

---

## Production Notes

- **Degradation state:** Heavy wear. Suits show sustained environmental damage — soot ground into panel seams, scuffed knee and elbow plating, patched tears (Jink's copper-wire forearm patch visible on others now too). Holographic nodes still pulse through grime but dimmer, flickering at the edges. Luma's suit is ink-stained at the hands and wrists from document work; Thresh's ruby armor has the long chest-plate scratch from Issue 10's telegraph fight plus accumulated dents. Wing-packs function but emit visible particles on deployment — degraded projection lattice. The suits look lived-in. The light endures.
- **Lighting baseline:** This issue uses four distinct lighting environments — camp interior (warm gaslight bleeding through wall gaps + low holographic glow), nighttime alley (single warm gaslight source, deep shadow), church basement (total darkness broken only by Luma's scanner blue-violet), and exterior night streets (fog-diffused gaslight amber). The church basement sequence is the visual centerpiece — scanner light is the only illumination source for eight pages.
- **Dialogue style:** Luma is precise, scientific, increasingly shaken. Sable is controlled — too controlled. Wren is observant, clipped. Theodore speaks period-appropriate Victorian English. Thresh speaks in fragments or not at all. The human child does not speak.
- **Key narrative plants:** Sable's overly precise questions (Pages 1-4) confirm she already knew about prior operatives. Wren notices this — setup for Issue 9 confrontation. The word "STAY" is the central image and will echo through the remaining four issues.

## SECTION 1: THE BRIEFING (Pages 1–4)

### Lighting Baseline
Camp interior. Warm amber gaslight bleeds through the wall gap, casting long shadows across timber beams. The squad's holographic perimeter markers provide low blue-white accents at the edges. Luma's presentation display is the dominant cool light — structured blue-violet, casting her data across the faces of the squad. The contrast between the warm camp and the cold data is the visual tension. When Sable asks her too-precise questions, her face is lit primarily by the holographic display — clinical light revealing controlled features.

### Environment Setup
The squad's wall-cavity camp, now well-established — gear stowed in organized positions on timber beams, a power cell providing dim ambient light, holographic perimeter markers at the entry gap. The space is cramped but organized. Military professionals making a home in the walls. Cobwebs pushed back but not eliminated. A gaslight from the room beyond the wall bleeds warm amber through the entry gap.
