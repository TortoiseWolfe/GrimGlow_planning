# GRIMGLOW Issue 11 — "Convergence" — Panel-by-Panel Production Script

## Production Notes

- **Degradation state:** Heavily degraded. All suits show the cumulative damage of ten issues — soot, scuffs, dents, tears, and now improvised repairs using scavenged Victorian materials. Wren's copper suit is patched with brass wire at the left forearm, oil stains permanent, copper dust embedded in the knees. Jink's emerald suit has a copper wire-stitched tear along one forearm and soot streaked across the chest, plus a new scuff along the right hip from the telegraph office fight. Thresh's crimson armor is dented at the left pauldron, scratched across the chest plate, and now bears a fresh gouge along the right vambrace from the Titan encounter — holographic edge-lighting stutters unevenly. Sable's silver suit is darkened with soot and shows a cracked panel at the right shoulder that has been sealed with a strip of scavenged tin; command presence persists through the damage. Luma's sapphire-violet suit shows dust, ink stains at the hands, a cracked holographic lens (one of two), and a new abrasion along the left calf. All suits: holographic elements glow through grime — light endures. The visual irony of this issue: the more battered the suits become, the more they look like fairy tales. Improvised repairs using copper wire, brass scraps, and scavenged material make the future soldiers look like creatures from folklore.
- **Lighting baseline:** Four distinct lighting environments — see section headers for specifics. The dominant palette shifts from cool holographic blue-white for analysis and planning scenes, to rooftop moonlight and gaslight for the character moments, to warm amber dual-lighting for preparations, and finally to warm lamplight for the folklorist's study. The Jink/Theodore rooftop scene uses the most balanced warm/cool dual-lighting in the volume — moonlight from above, gaslight from below, Jink's wing-pack seam glow the only fairy light.
- **Dialogue style:** This issue balances military professionalism with emotional intimacy. Wren is precise and technical in the briefing — she delivers the analysis like an engineer reading a catastrophic system report. Sable returns to command mode for the decision — clipped, no contractions, the mask back on for a purpose she believes in. The planning sequence is pure military: roles, targets, timing. The character moments break the register entirely — Jink serious for the first time, speaking without quips; Theodore stubborn and formal in his Victorian cadence; Luma quiet with recognition; Thresh wordless but present; Sable closing the file without ceremony.
- **Key narrative beats:** Wren's temporal nudge engine analysis (Pages 1-5). Sable's decision to intervene (Pages 6-8). Operational planning (Pages 9-11). Character moments: Jink/Theodore rooftop (Pages 12-14), Luma at the church cache (Page 15), Thresh with her weapon (Page 15-16), Sable closing the file (Page 16). Final preparations (Pages 17-20). Night before (Page 21). Fairy tale echo — James Machen's pamphlet (Page 22).
- **Panel count target:** ~95 panels across 22 pages.

## SECTION 1: WREN'S ANALYSIS (Pages 1–5)

### Lighting Baseline
Cool holographic copper-gold dominates the briefing space. Wren's analysis projections are the primary light source — copper-gold schematics of telegraph networks, amber-black markers for Titan device locations, flowing data streams in geometric patterns. The camp's perimeter markers are dim (power cells further depleted since Issue 9). Thin grey daylight seeps through the wall gap — morning. The Titan device under analysis pulses with its own sickly amber-black light, a dark counterpoint to Wren's clean holographic schematics. The lighting is clinical and urgent — the light of diagnosis, not comfort.

### Scene Setup
Morning after the Titan encounter. The squad is gathered at the main briefing area — the central beam where Sable and Wren faced each other in Issue 9. Now Wren commands the space. The captured Titan device sits on a beam section under Wren's scanner array, pulsing with amber-black light that makes the surrounding fairy tech flicker. Wren's holographic projections fill the air above the squad — a copper-gold schematic of the city's telegraph network overlaid with amber-black markers showing Titan device locations. The squad is reunited by purpose — fractured in Issue 9, pulled together by the threat revealed in Issue 10.
