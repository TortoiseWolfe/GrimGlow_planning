# GRIMGLOW Issue 9 — "Sable's File" — Panel-by-Panel Production Script

---

## Production Notes

- **Degradation state:** Significantly degraded. All suits show accumulated damage from eight issues of exposure — soot, scuffs, dents, improvised repairs, environmental staining. Wren's copper suit is the most worn (oil-stained forearms, copper dust ground into the knees, scorch mark on shoulder). Jink's emerald suit has a patched tear along one forearm (copper wire stitching), soot streaked across the chest. Thresh's crimson armor is dented at the left pauldron, scratched across the chest plate, holographic edge-lighting stuttering unevenly. Sable's silver suit is scuffed and darkened with soot but still carries authority — the command presence persists through the damage. Luma's sapphire-violet suit is the least physically damaged but shows dust, ink stains at the hands, and a cracked holographic lens (one of two). Holographic elements on all suits glow through grime — light endures. The visual metaphor: the squad is falling apart, and so are their suits.
- **Lighting baseline:** This issue uses four distinct lighting environments — see section headers for specifics. The dominant palette is cool holographic blue-white for confrontation and revelation scenes, darkness and isolation for the aftermath, warm gaslight for Theodore's visit, and cold data-module uplight for the final page.
- **Dialogue style:** This is the most dialogue-heavy issue in the volume. Every voice must be distinct. Sable reports truth like a briefing even as the mask cracks — no contractions in command mode, contractions when the mask slips. Wren is systematic, diagnostic, factual — not cruel. Jink is sharp, fast, wounded — the quips stop when the betrayal lands. Thresh barely speaks — operational vocabulary compressed past silence. Luma connects, asks questions that reframe. Theodore speaks in period-appropriate Victorian English — earnest, formal, the cadence of oral storytelling when he tells the fairy tale.
- **Key narrative beats:** Wren confronts Sable (Pages 1-4). Mission file revealed to squad (Pages 5-10). Squad fractures (Pages 11-16). Aftermath isolation (Pages 17-19). Theodore's fairy tale (Pages 20-21). Sable alone — the mask fully off (Page 22).
- **Panel count target:** ~95 panels across 22 pages.

## SECTION 1: THE CONFRONTATION (Pages 1–4)

### Lighting Baseline
Cool holographic blue-white dominates. The camp's perimeter lights are low — power cells dimming after eight issues of use. The primary light source during the confrontation is Sable's data module display, which casts structured geometric light upward across both faces. Background camp lighting is warm amber from the thin gaslight seeping through the wall gap — but it is distant, peripheral. The emotional temperature of the light is clinical, exposing, nowhere to hide.

### Scene Setup
The confrontation happens on a narrow wooden beam in the wall cavity, away from where the rest of the squad sleeps. Wren has chosen the time deliberately — pre-dawn, the others not yet awake. The space is tight and intimate. The beam runs between two vertical timbers, cobwebs in the corners, nails the size of bollards protruding from the wood. Dust motes float in the faint camp light.
