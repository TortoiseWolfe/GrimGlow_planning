# GRIMGLOW — Volume 1: *The Stories They Tell* — Cover Art Prompts

## Cover Design Notes

**Trade dress:** Title "GRIMGLOW" across top third in geometric sans-serif with faint holographic prismatic effect. Subtitle and issue number below title. Leave the top 25% of composition as negative space or atmospheric gradient for title placement.

**Consistent elements:** Each cover features at least one squad member at fairy scale (4 inches tall) against a Victorian environment. The degradation arc is visible in suit condition. Dual lighting (warm gaslight vs. cool holographic) present in every cover. Oil paint texture, visible brushwork throughout.

**Color palette progression:** Covers shift from cool/pristine (Issue 1) through warm/amber (Issues 3-5) to dark/battle-worn (Issues 9-11) to warm candlelight (Issue 12). The arc is visible across a shelf of spines.

## Issue 1 — "Descent"

```text
Painterly illustrated cover art, rich detail. COVER COMPOSITION — leave top 25% as dark night sky for title. Center focal point: a tiny woman approximately four inches tall stands at the very edge of a Victorian rooftop rain gutter, silhouetted against a vast gaslit city stretching to the horizon below. Dark brown skin, close-cropped silver-white hair catching moonlight, pristine metallic silver suit with blue-white light bleeding from every seam. She stands with command posture — weight forward, shoulders set — looking DOWN into the enormity of the world she has crashed into. Behind and below her, the gutter holds the smoking wreckage of alien technology — twisted metal, dying holographic sparks. Above, filling the title space, a streak of prismatic light fades across the night sky — the falling star. The city below is a canyon of amber gaslight, church spires, chimney forests, fog layers. The scale is the cover: one four-inch woman against all of Victorian London. Warm amber city-glow rising from below, cool blue-white from her suit and the dying wreckage. Oil paint texture, visible brushwork, dramatic vertical composition, awe and isolation.
```

## Issue 10 — "The Titan"

## Issue 12 — "The Stories They Tell"

```text
Painterly illustrated cover art, rich detail. COVER COMPOSITION — leave top 25% as warm dark workshop shadows for title, candlelight bleeding upward. Center focal point: a boy's hands on an open journal. Steel-nib pen in the right hand, brass gear ring catching candlelight on the left index finger. On the journal page, partially visible in a boy's careful handwriting: the beginning of a story about beings of light with wings like dragonflies made of coloured fire. Ink on his fingertips. The boy's face is above the hands, softly lit — sandy-brown hair, hazel eyes focused downward, freckles, expression quiet and tender. He is writing about his friends and does not know he is writing mythology. Scattered across the workbench around the journal: clockwork tools, brass gears, and traces of the fairy squad — a tiny holographic emitter still faintly glowing with geometric light in varied hues, a miniature engineering tool, a scrap of metallic suit material catching candlelight. The fairies are absent. Their light endures. A single candle provides all illumination — warm amber dominating entirely, the faint holographic glow from the emitter the last whisper of cool fairy-tech light in the volume. No other light sources. The workshop is the same one from Issue 3, but the boy is different now. Warm amber candlelight, Vermeer intimacy, oil paint texture, visible brushwork — an ending that is also a beginning. The first words of the oldest fairy tale.
```
