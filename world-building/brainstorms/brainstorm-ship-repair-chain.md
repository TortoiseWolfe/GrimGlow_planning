Theodore is "apprentice to a clockmaker named Mr. Cribbage" (Plot Outline). His access to workshop materials and the Clerkenwell trade network makes him a supply chain for lower-risk components.

### What Theodore Can Provide

- <AI>Brass fittings — Cribbage's shop would stock brass components as standard clockmaker's supplies</AI>
- Victorian hand tools for Wren — "Files, loupes, pliers, jeweller's screwdrivers. Wren acquires these from Theodore's workshop." (steampunk tech brainstorm)

## 5. The Degradation Factor

### Wren's Toolkit Degrades

Canon: fairy tech degrades across the volume. "The suits start pristine in Issue 1 and accumulate damage, soot, and improvised repairs. By Issue 12, the squad looks like what they've become." (Plot Outline)

Wren's tools specifically: "Holographic cutting beams, molecular scanners, precision calibration interfaces. These work but are getting less reliable (degradation arc)." (steampunk tech brainstorm)

<AI>"Early heists: Wren's tools work perfectly, adaptation is straightforward. Late heists: her scanner glitches, her cutting beam drifts, she has to improvise with Victorian hand tools. The adaptation process itself degrades." (steampunk tech brainstorm)</AI>

### The Four-Phase Adaptation Process

From steampunk tech brainstorm, Section 5 — per component:

1. **Identify:** Wren scans the ship's damaged system, identifies the functional requirement
2. **Translate:** Maps that requirement to Victorian technology — material, precision, form
3. **Locate:** Identifies where in London the best-quality equivalent can be found
4. **Acquire → Modify → Interface → Test:** The heist, the reshaping, the holographic coupling, the verification

"Wren's job is not to rebuild fairy tech from scratch. It's to provide the mechanical skeleton that her holographic systems can interface with." (steampunk tech brainstorm)
